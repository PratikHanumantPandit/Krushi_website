Drip Irrigation

An efficient water-saving method that allows water to drip slowly to the roots of plants, either from above the soil surface or buried below. This technique minimizes evaporation and ensures that water is used exactly where it's needed, making it ideal for water-scarce regions in India.

Organic Farming

This approach relies on natural methods like crop rotation, green manure, compost, and biological pest control. It avoids the use of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, which promotes biodiversity, improves soil fertility, and produces healthier food.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is a sustainable and eco-friendly approach to managing pests. It combines biological, cultural, physical, and chemical tools to minimize economic, health, and environmental risks. It focuses on pest prevention and uses pesticides only as a last resort.

Crop Rotation

The practice of growing a series of different types of crops in the same area across a sequence of growing seasons. This method improves soil health by managing soil nutrients, reduces soil erosion, and helps control pests and weeds by breaking their cycles.

Zero Tillage Farming

A conservation agriculture technique where crops are planted directly into the soil without ploughing or tilling. This minimizes soil disturbance, which helps increase water retention, reduce erosion, and improve soil organic matter over time.
