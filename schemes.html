Government Schemes for Farmers

Supporting the backbone of our nation with key policies and financial aid.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

This central scheme provides direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to all landholding farmer families. The amount is transferred in three equal installments of ₹2,000 directly into their bank accounts to supplement their financial needs for procuring various inputs related to agriculture and allied activities as well as domestic needs.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

PMFBY is the government-sponsored crop insurance scheme that integrates multiple stakeholders on a single platform. It provides comprehensive insurance coverage against crop failure due to non-preventable natural risks, thus helping to stabilise the income of farmers and encourage them to adopt innovative practices.

Soil Health Card Scheme

This scheme aims to issue 'Soil Health Cards' to farmers which will carry crop-wise recommendations of nutrients and fertilizers required for the individual farms to help farmers to improve productivity through judicious use of inputs. It promotes soil testing and balanced use of fertilizers to maintain the health of the soil.

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

Introduced in 1998, the KCC scheme aims at providing adequate and timely credit support from the banking system under a single window to the farmers for their cultivation and other needs. It helps farmers purchase agricultural inputs like seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides, and also allows them to withdraw funds for their other production needs.

National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)

e-NAM is a pan-India electronic trading portal which networks the existing APMC mandis to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities. It aims to provide more competitive and transparent price discovery for farmers for their produce through an online, competitive bidding process.
